Figure Sculpture

Figurative sculpture is to sculpture as drawing is to painting. Through the disciplined study of the beautiful and the sublime in nature it trains the mind to visualize complex forms in 3 dimensions.

The goal of this yearly series of sculptures intensives is to preserve and transmit the time honored fine art techniques of classical figure sculpture. To preserve and transmit such techniques and to future generations specifically and to raise the overall level of sculptural expression in general.

7-Day Workshops

Figure Sculpture Intensive 1: The Portrait Bust

$650 ($300 for continuing students)

Available Sessions

  • June 7 - 13
  • August 2 - 8
  • January 3 - 9

Description

This is 7-day, all-day figure sculpture intensive workshop includes work with a live model. Students will learn the fundamentals of sculpting the classical portrait bust plus the basic mold-making technique. Classes run 6 hours, from 9:30 to 3:30 daily for 7 days. Each class is divided into 4.5 hours of supervised sculpting time, two 30 min lectures, and lunch.

In this intensive workshop students will learn

  • basic clay sculpture technique
  • basic facial anatomy and proportion
  • armature construction
  • basic composition of the portrait bust
  • sculpture clay bodies and their use
  • plaster types and their use
  • basic waste molding technique

Class schedule and lecture topics

Day 1 - Armature construction, sculpture tools and techniques
Day 2 - Facial anatomy, proportion, and the trick to capturing the likeness
Day 3 - Clay bodies, systems of 3-dimensional composition, square roots and the golden mean
Day 4 - Anatomy: Eyes, lips, and ears
Day 5 - Casting vs firing, molding systems and their uses
Day 6 - Shimming, plaster types and basic molding material
Day 7 - Hollowing technique, plaster mixing and application, mold removal

Materials supplied include

  • 1 bag of plaster
  • 50 pounds of clay
  • 1 head armature
  • Duct tape and styrofoam

2-Day Workshops

2 Day Figure Life Sculpture Intensive

Class Limit: 4
2 days - 12 hours
$150 per student

Available Sessions

  • June 13 & 20
  • July 18 & 25
  • August 22 & 29
  • October 10 & 17
  • December 12 & 19

Supplies

Model fees are included in tuition. Students are expected to supply a sketchbook, drawing pencil, and a 25-lbs bag of terracotta clay.

Description

This figure sculpture intensive workshop is for both beginners and intermediate sculptors. Students will explore the classical techniques for rendering the figure in three dimensions from a live model. This class is kept small to ensure intimate, individual attention in the class environment. It will focus on the development of observational skills, clay working technique, anatomy, proportions, and preparing the sculpture for firing.

Class schedule and lecture topics

Day 1 - Session One - Getting Started (3 hours)
This session will focus on different techniques for “starting” a sculpture. Topics covered will be posing the model and the use of different systems for visually encompassing the “whole” figure so as to compose the sculpture in three dimensions.

Day 1 - Session Two - Landmarks (3 hours)
This session will deal with anatomical landmark recognition. There are basic anatomical structural landmarks on the body which communicate proportions, center of gravity and the gestural line. These points encapsulate the dynamism and feeling of the pose.

Day 2 - Session Three - Practical Application (6 hours)
In this 6-hour session students will put their lesson from the prior day into practice by working from the live model to carry a sculpture through to completion. Preparation for firing will be dealt with in the last hour of class.
